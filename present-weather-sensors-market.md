<p><p>Present weather sensors are vital for detecting and reporting weather conditions. The market for these sensors primarily includes optical and ultrasonic types. Optical sensors utilize light-based technology to identify weather phenomena, such as rain, snow, or fog, by analyzing the scattering of light. In contrast, ultrasonic sensors employ sound waves to measure precipitation intensity and type, functioning by emitting ultrasonic pulses that detect changes in air pressure and movement. Both types are essential for accurate and real-time weather monitoring.</p></p>
