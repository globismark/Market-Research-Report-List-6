<p><p>The refractory materials market is categorized into three types based on their service temperatures. Common refractory materials, with temperatures ranging from 1580℃ to 1770℃, are widely used in industrial applications like steelmaking. Advanced refractory materials, operating between 1770℃ and 2000℃, offer enhanced properties for more demanding environments, such as in glass and ceramics production. Superrefractory materials, exceeding 2000℃, are utilized in extreme high-temperature applications, including aerospace and advanced energy systems, due to their exceptional thermal stability and durability.</p></p>
