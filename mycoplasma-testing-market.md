<p><p>The mycoplasma testing market encompasses various methods for detecting mycoplasma contamination in cell cultures and biological products. PCR (Polymerase Chain Reaction) offers high sensitivity and specificity for identifying mycoplasma DNA. ELSA (Enzyme-Linked Staphylococcal Assay) provides an enzyme-linked immunosorbent option for detecting mycoplasma proteins. Enzymatic methods utilize specific enzymatic reactions to identify mycoplasma presence, while DNA staining techniques involve the application of dyes that bind to DNA, allowing for visualization of contaminated cells under a microscope. Each method offers distinct advantages depending on the testing needs.</p></p>
