<p><p>The seed treatments market is divided into two primary types: chemical and non-chemical treatments. Chemical seed treatments involve the application of synthetic pesticides or fungicides to seeds, protecting them from pests, diseases, and improving germination rates. Non-chemical seed treatments utilize biological agents, natural extracts, or physical methods such as hot water or steam treatments, focusing on sustainable practices and reducing chemical residues. Both types aim to enhance seed performance and crop yield while addressing specific agricultural challenges.</p></p>
