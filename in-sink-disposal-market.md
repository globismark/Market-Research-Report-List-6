<p><p>The in-sink disposal market consists of two main types: batch feed and continuous feed. Batch feed disposals require users to load food waste into the unit and secure a lid before operation, promoting safety and minimizing accidental activation. Continuous feed disposals allow users to add waste while the unit is running, making it more convenient for continuous cooking processes. Each type caters to different consumer preferences and kitchen workflows, impacting their popularity and market share.</p></p>
